About me

Currently I’m seeking new opportunities in full stack web development and to further my existing skills with Linux systems. I’ve been participating in online courses to better understand full stack development for building websites from the ground up. I decided to move into web development after seven years of providing technical support in client-facing and B2B circumstances. My various support roles have allowed me opportunities to test and upgrade software, support large data centers, administrate user accounts, and plan server implementations in live production environments.

I find great enjoyment in making things that work efficiently and intuitively. My experience helping customers has taught me that sometimes an application lacks user friendliness, even if it is state-of-the-art software. It’s this exact experience that I want to use to design and build websites that will make sense to their target audience. I want to help bridge the gap that exists between brilliantly built applications and their users so that the user experience will be nothing less than exceptional.
